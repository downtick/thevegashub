Why Bother for a Vegas Trip?

Most people skip travel insurance for domestic trips and assume nothing will go wrong. Then a flight gets cancelled the night before a concert, or someone ends up in an urgent care after a slip by the pool — and suddenly a $200 policy looks very affordable.

Vegas trips often involve non-refundable hotel deposits, show tickets, tours booked in advance, and flights. That's real money on the line. A standard trip protection plan covers most of it for a fraction of what you'd lose.

What a Good Plan Covers

✈

Trip Cancellation & Interruption

Reimburses prepaid, non-refundable expenses if you cancel for a covered reason — illness, injury, family emergency, job loss, or severe weather. Trip interruption covers you if you have to cut the trip short and come home early.

🏥

Emergency Medical & Evacuation

Covers hospital bills if you get sick or injured during your trip. Medical evacuation — getting you home if you can't fly commercially — can cost $50,000+ without coverage. This is the most important benefit for international visitors.

🧳

Baggage Loss & Delay

Compensates for lost, stolen, or damaged bags. Baggage delay coverage pays for essentials — clothes, toiletries — while you wait for bags that didn't make your flight.

⏱

Travel Delay

If your flight is delayed by 6+ hours, this covers meals, hotels, and transport while you wait. Very useful during peak Vegas weekends when flights are overbooked and weather disruptions cascade.

🔄

Cancel for Any Reason (CFAR)

An optional upgrade that lets you cancel for literally any reason — not just covered ones — and get back 50–75% of your trip cost. Worth adding if you booked a big trip and life feels uncertain.

INTERNATIONAL VISITORS

Visiting the USA from Outside the Country?

US healthcare is world-class — and world-expensive. A single ER visit can run $3,000–$10,000. Your home country's national health plan almost certainly does not cover you in the United States, and most US health insurers won't touch a foreign visitor's claim.

Visitors to USA insurance fills that gap. It's designed specifically for non-US citizens traveling to America, covering emergency medical, hospitalization, urgent care, and evacuation — often for just a few dollars a day.

A Few Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Buy within 14–21 days of your first trip deposit to unlock the most coverage options, including pre-existing condition waivers and CFAR.
  • Insure your total trip cost — if you only insure part of it, you'll only recover part of it.
  • Check whether your credit card already provides some trip coverage before buying a standalone policy — some premium cards do.
  • Read the "covered reasons" list before purchase. Policies vary significantly.
  • International visitors: purchase before you leave home. Some plans cannot be purchased after you've already entered the US.
